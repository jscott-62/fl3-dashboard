## The Angle

The traditional financial advice industry isn't just giving bad advice. It's running a playbook designed to benefit advisors, not clients. The AUM fee model creates a structural incentive to keep your money parked, not to help you build wealth. Once you see the game, you can't unsee it.

---

## Key Points (3-5)

1. **The AUM incentive problem.** Advisors charge 1% of assets under management. They get paid when you stay, not when you win. This misalignment drives everything else.
2. **The Three Moves.** The industry uses three predictable moves to keep clients locked in: the Complexity Shield (making investing sound impossibly hard), the Risk Scare (everything else is "too risky"), and the Time Delay ("you still have time").
3. **The real math on purchasing power.** At 7% returns minus 1.5% fees minus 8% real inflation, your purchasing power shrinks by ~2.5% every year. The quarterly statement still looks green, but you're losing ground.
4. **Caution weaponized.** For the Pursuer (someone who researches thoroughly before acting), the industry exploits that instinct by feeding them just enough complexity to keep researching forever without ever looking elsewhere.
5. **The question that changes everything.** "What are the incentives behind this recommendation?" Ask your advisor that at the next meeting. Their response tells you everything.
6. Financial advisors aren't bad people, they are just playing from an old playbook

## Key Stats / Data

- 1% AUM management fee + 0.5% fund expenses = 1.5% total fees
- 7% average returns minus 1.5% fees = 5.5% real return
- 5.5% returns minus 8% real inflation = negative 2.5% purchasing power per year
- $300,000 portfolio example: losing purchasing power every year while statements still show green
- Traditional planning industry built in 1970s/80s, hasn't fundamentally changed

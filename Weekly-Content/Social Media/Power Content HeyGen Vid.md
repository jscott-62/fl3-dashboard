Here's something that made me angry when I finally understood it:

Your "safe" investments aren't safe. They're just dying slowly instead of quickly.

A savings account losing money each year to inflation feels safe. Kind of.

A stock market crash losing 30% in a month feels dangerous.

But here's the math no one talks about:

Exposure to inflation is a certain, definite loss every single year. And it's way more than the media or finance dudes tell you.

A market crash: might happen, might not, markets recover

We've been trained to fear the visible risk and ignore the invisible one.

The invisible one is the one that's actually guaranteed to hurt you.

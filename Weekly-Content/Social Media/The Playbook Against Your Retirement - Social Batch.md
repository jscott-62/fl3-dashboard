## Post 3: LinkedIn (Authority + Insight)

**Format:** Text post

I want to talk about something uncomfortable.

The traditional financial advice industry was built in the 1970s and 80s. The world has changed. The advice hasn't.

Your advisor charges 1% AUM. Your funds charge another 0.5%. That's 1.5% off the top, every year, win or lose.

At 7% average returns and 8%+ real inflation, you're actually losing purchasing power. Slowly. Quietly. While your quarterly statement still shows green.

Three things keep people locked in:

1. Complexity that makes you feel dependent
2. Fear of anything outside the recommended portfolio
3. Constant delay ("you still have time")

The wealthiest investors aren't in balanced funds earning 7%. They're in asymmetric assets with completely different risk/reward profiles.

I'm not saying fire your advisor tomorrow. I'm saying ask one question at your next meeting:

"What are the incentives behind this recommendation?"

That question changes everything.

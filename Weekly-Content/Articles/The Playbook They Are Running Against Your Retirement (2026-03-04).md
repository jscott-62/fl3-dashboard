# The Playbook They're Running Against Your Retirement (And What This Bear Market Actually Means)

Bitcoin is down. The headlines are ugly. And if you've been watching the markets over the past few months, you might be feeling that familiar knot in your stomach.

Here's what I want you to know: that knot is exactly what the system is counting on.

Because while everyone is fixated on Bitcoin's short-term price action, something far more significant is happening behind the scenes. The same playbook that governments used in the 1940s to erase the national debt is being deployed right now. And if you don't understand how it works, your retirement savings are the ones funding it.

**This isn't a conspiracy theory. It's documented policy. And it changes everything about how you should think about "safe" investing.**

## The Bear Market Is Real. So Is the Pattern.

Let's start with honesty. Bitcoin is in a bear market.

Analyst Benjamin Cowen has been tracking this for months, and the data is striking. Bitcoin topped in Q4 of 2025, just as it has in Q4 of every post-halving year: 2013, 2017, 2021. The pattern is almost eerie in its consistency.

February brought the expected lows. March brought the expected rally. As of early March, Bitcoin surged about 7% in a single day, and it's currently sitting roughly 15 to 16% below its yearly open. That's almost exactly where it sat at this point in 2014, 2018, and 2022.

Cowen's base case: this rally gets rejected, Bitcoin forms a lower high, and we head lower before finding a more durable bottom later this year. Bear markets make fools of both bulls and bears. Some of the most face-ripping rallies happen in the middle of them.

If you're panicking, stop. If you're celebrating, slow down. The cycle is doing what cycles do. The question isn't whether this bear market is real. It's what you do with the time it gives you.

## The Invisible Tax Nobody Voted For

Here's where it gets personal.

The International Monetary Fund published a paper documenting exactly how governments erase debt through what they call financial repression. The mechanism is simple: keep interest rates below the real rate of inflation. The gap between what your savings earn and what inflation costs you is a silent transfer of wealth from you to the government.

Mark Moss broke this down this week, and the numbers are brutal. After World War II, US debt was at 120% of GDP. By keeping bond yields at 2.5% while inflation ran as high as 18%, the government liquidated that debt from 120% down to 35% over three decades. They didn't pay it back. They inflated it away.

Today, US debt is at 123% of GDP. Nearly identical starting point. And the same four options exist: run a surplus (not happening), default (off the table), grow faster than the debt (uncertain), or inflate it away.

Guess which one they're choosing.

The Fed is cutting rates. Real inflation in food, healthcare, and housing is running well above official numbers. And every dollar sitting in bonds, CDs, or a 60/40 portfolio is quietly being liquidated to fund the deleveraging. The portion of your portfolio that's supposed to be "safe" is the exact portion being eroded by design.

This isn't volatility. This is policy.

## Wall Street Wants What You Have

Meanwhile, something else is happening that should get your attention. Wall Street is fighting to control Bitcoin.

Andrei Jikh documented how Jane Street, one of the most powerful trading firms on the planet, is one of only four authorized participants for BlackRock's Bitcoin ETF. They don't have to disclose their short positions, options, or derivatives. They can create and destroy ETF shares with privileges no retail investor has. And they were found guilty by India's securities regulator of manipulating that country's stock market using a strategy that looks almost identical to what people have been accusing them of doing to Bitcoin.

For months, Bitcoin dropped 2 to 3% at exactly 10:00 AM Eastern every trading day. The moment a lawsuit against Jane Street became public, the pattern stopped. Bitcoin surged 10%. Two hundred billion dollars re-entered the crypto market in 48 hours.

Here's what matters: Wall Street isn't fighting over things that don't have value. The fact that sophisticated firms are building complex infrastructure around Bitcoin tells you everything you need to know about where the smart money thinks this is going long-term. They just don't want you there before they're positioned.

## The Political Machine Is Lining Up

The crypto industry has raised $228 million for the 2026 midterm elections. Fairshake, the industry's main super PAC, has amassed $193 million alone, with Coinbase, Ripple, and Andreessen Horowitz each contributing roughly $50 million. In 2024, 85% of Fairshake-backed candidates won. An estimated 270 pro-crypto lawmakers now sit in Congress.

The Clarity Act, which would define clear regulatory frameworks for crypto, passed the House with bipartisan support and is now being considered in the Senate. Banks are fighting it because stablecoin yield provisions could pull trillions from their balance sheets. Bank of America's CEO warned that $6.6 trillion could exit banks if stablecoins are allowed to offer yield.

Think about that for a moment. The banking industry is terrified that people will move their money into a better system. That terror is telling you something about where the value is migrating.

The regulatory landscape isn't perfect. It's messy, political, and increasingly partisan. But the trajectory is clear: Bitcoin and crypto are becoming embedded in the financial and political infrastructure of the United States. That doesn't happen to assets that are going away.

## The Real Risk Is the One You Can't See

Most people think risk means volatility. Price goes down, that's risky. Price stays flat, that's safe.

That definition is killing your retirement.

The real risk isn't Bitcoin dropping 20% in a bear market. The real risk is sitting in assets that earn 5 to 7% while inflation eats 8 to 14% of your purchasing power every year. For decades. The risk isn't the thing that moves. It's the thing that doesn't move enough.

If you're 50 years old with $200,000 saved, earning 7% in an index fund, and real inflation is running at 8%, you are going backward every single year. The math doesn't work. Not because you made bad decisions, but because you were given the wrong vehicle for the race you're actually in.

Erin Talks Money pointed out this week that mortgage rates are around 6%, and the Fed may cut once or twice this year. She made a critical distinction: the Fed controls the thermostat, but mortgage rates reflect the climate. The bond market is telling you that rates may come down gradually, but not dramatically. Translation: the environment of financial repression isn't ending anytime soon.

## What You Can Actually Do

This bear market isn't punishment. It's an invitation.

**First,** understand the cycle. Bitcoin's bear markets follow documented, repeatable patterns. They end. Every single time. The people who position themselves during the downturn are the ones who benefit from the recovery.

**Second,** question your "safe" allocation. If 40% of your portfolio is in bonds or bond-like instruments, you're actively funding the government's debt liquidation strategy. That's not safety. That's a guaranteed loss in real terms.

**Third,** if you're buying Bitcoin, consider self-custody. As Andrei Jikh emphasized, buying through an ETF means trusting the same system Bitcoin was designed to replace. Self-custody means no middleman can liquidate you, freeze your assets, or manipulate your holdings.

**Fourth,** use time, not timing. Whether you're dollar-cost averaging $50 a week or making a lump-sum move, the bear market is when accumulation matters most. The best returns go to people who bought when it felt the worst.

The system wasn't built for people who need to catch up. It was built to protect the wealth of people who already have it. But for the first time in history, there's a vehicle that can actually close the gap. If you're willing to look past the headlines and understand what's really happening.

The bear market is temporary. Financial repression is not. Choose your risk accordingly.
